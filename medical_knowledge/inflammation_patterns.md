# Inflammation Patterns Interpretation

## 1. Related Tests

* C-Reactive Protein (CRP)
* Erythrocyte Sedimentation Rate (ESR)
* White Blood Cell Count (WBC)

---

## 2. What High Values May Suggest

### High CRP

* Acute inflammation
* Infection
* Tissue injury
* Autoimmune or inflammatory disorders
* Response to significant physiological stress

### High ESR

* Chronic inflammation
* Autoimmune conditions
* Persistent infections
* Some inflammatory disorders
* Certain malignancy-associated inflammatory patterns

### High WBC Count

* Infection
* Inflammatory response
* Physiological stress
* Medication effects
* Bone marrow-related conditions

---

## 3. What Low Values May Suggest

### Low CRP

* Low level of detectable systemic inflammation
* Reduced inflammatory response in some clinical situations

### Low ESR

* Absence of significant inflammatory activity
* Certain blood-related conditions
* Reduced inflammatory protein levels

### Low WBC Count

* Reduced immune cell production
* Medication effects
* Viral infections
* Bone marrow suppression
* Immune system disorders

---

## 4. Common Supporting Symptoms

### Inflammation-Related

* Fever
* Fatigue
* General weakness
* Pain
* Swelling
* Redness or warmth in affected areas
* Loss of appetite
* Weight changes

### Infection-Related

* Chills
* Sweating
* Localized pain
* Cough or respiratory symptoms
* Urinary symptoms
* Gastrointestinal symptoms

---

## 5. Missing Evidence

* Patient history and symptom duration
* Physical examination findings
* Recent infections or injuries
* Medication history
* Previous laboratory results for comparison
* Additional diagnostic evaluation based on suspected cause
* Imaging or specialized testing when clinically indicated

---

## 6. Suggested Follow-up

* Review symptoms, duration, and severity
* Assess for signs of infection or inflammatory disease
* Review medications and recent medical events
* Repeat abnormal results when clinically appropriate
* Investigate possible underlying causes based on clinical presentation
* Consider referral to a specialist if abnormalities persist or are unexplained

---

## 7. Safety Note

This document is intended for clinical decision support only. Laboratory findings must be interpreted together with the patient's medical history, physical examination, and additional diagnostic information. It does not provide a final diagnosis, does not replace physician judgment, and should not be used as the sole basis for treatment decisions.
