# Anemia Patterns Interpretation

## 1. Related Tests

* Hemoglobin (Hb)
* Hematocrit (Hct)
* Red Blood Cell Count (RBC)
* Mean Corpuscular Volume (MCV)
* Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin (MCH)
* Mean Corpuscular Hemoglobin Concentration (MCHC)
* Red Cell Distribution Width (RDW)

---

## 2. What High Values May Suggest

### High Hemoglobin and Hematocrit

* Increased red blood cell concentration
* Dehydration-related blood concentration changes
* Chronic low oxygen states
* Increased red blood cell production disorders

### High MCV

* Macrocytic anemia patterns
* Vitamin deficiency-related changes
* Alcohol-related effects
* Certain medication effects

### High RDW

* Increased variation in red blood cell size
* Mixed anemia patterns
* Developing nutritional deficiencies

---

## 3. What Low Values May Suggest

### Low Hemoglobin and Hematocrit

* Anemia
* Blood loss
* Reduced red blood cell production
* Increased red blood cell destruction

### Low RBC Count

* Reduced red blood cell production
* Blood loss
* Nutritional deficiency-related anemia

### Low MCV

* Microcytic anemia patterns
* Iron deficiency patterns
* Certain inherited hemoglobin disorders

### Low MCH or MCHC

* Reduced hemoglobin content inside red blood cells
* Iron deficiency-related patterns

---

## 4. Common Supporting Symptoms

### Anemia-Related

* Fatigue
* Weakness
* Shortness of breath
* Dizziness
* Pale skin
* Rapid heartbeat
* Reduced exercise tolerance
* Headache
* Cold hands and feet

### Possible Severe Symptoms

* Chest discomfort
* Fainting
* Confusion
* Severe weakness

---

## 5. Missing Evidence

* Clinical history including bleeding, diet, and chronic diseases
* Medication review
* Physical examination
* Previous blood count results for comparison
* Additional laboratory evaluation to identify the cause of anemia
* Assessment of nutritional status when indicated

---

## 6. Suggested Follow-up

* Review symptoms and medical history
* Assess possible sources of blood loss
* Review dietary intake and medications
* Repeat abnormal blood count results when appropriate
* Perform additional diagnostic evaluation based on the anemia pattern
* Consider referral to a hematologist for persistent, severe, or unexplained anemia

---

## 7. Safety Note

This document is intended for clinical decision support only. Laboratory findings must be interpreted together with the patient's medical history, physical examination, and additional diagnostic information. It does not provide a final diagnosis, does not replace physician judgment, and should not be used as the sole basis for treatment decisions.
