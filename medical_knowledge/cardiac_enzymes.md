# Cardiac Enzymes Clinical Review

## Related Tests

- Troponin (TnI, TnT)
- CPK (CK, creatine kinase, creatine phosphokinase)

## High Troponin

Elevated troponin supports review for myocardial injury in the full clinical context. The value alone does not establish a cause. Serial values, symptoms, ECG findings, renal function, timing, and assay-specific limits may be important missing evidence.

## High CPK or CK

Elevated CPK, also called CK or creatine kinase, supports review for skeletal or cardiac muscle injury context. Recent strenuous exercise, muscle trauma, seizures, injections, medication exposure, and other muscle stressors can affect CPK. Total CPK is not specific for myocardial injury.

## CPK With Normal Troponin

High CPK with a troponin value within the configured range should not be treated as equivalent to elevated troponin. Review the timing of measurements, muscle symptoms, exercise or trauma history, medication history, renal function, and any available CK fraction or repeat laboratory evidence.

## Supporting Symptoms

Chest pain, shortness of breath, sweating, nausea, muscle pain, or weakness may provide context but do not determine the source of an enzyme elevation.

## Missing Evidence

Useful review evidence may include serial troponin and CPK values, ECG findings, symptom timing, examination findings, recent exertion or trauma, medication exposure, and relevant renal measurements.

## Safety Note

This material is educational clinical decision support. It supports clinician review and does not provide a final diagnosis or prescribing instruction.
